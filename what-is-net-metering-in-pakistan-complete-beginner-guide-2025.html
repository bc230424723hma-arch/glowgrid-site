What Is Net Metering in Pakistan? Complete Beginner Guide (2025)

20256 min read

Net metering in Pakistan allows solar users to export extra electricity to the grid and receive bill credits. In simple terms, your solar system powers your home first; surplus units are sent to the utility network through a bi-directional meter.

How Net Metering Works

During daylight, if your solar generation is more than your current load, the extra units are exported. At night, you import from the grid. Your final bill reflects the net balance.

Who Can Apply?

  • Residential consumers with eligible sanctioned load
  • Commercial users with compliant solar installation
  • Consumers whose systems meet utility standards and safety requirements

Main Requirements

Requirements vary by DISCO, but generally include system design documents, ownership/connection records, approved inverter/panel specs, and technical inspection readiness.

Why Net Metering Matters

Without net metering, excess daytime power is often underutilized. With it, system economics improve significantly, especially for users with lower daytime demand.

Quick Tip: Choose an installer that handles documentation and utility coordination end-to-end to avoid delays.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Installing oversized systems without proper usage analysis
  2. Using non-compliant equipment
  3. Ignoring safety protections and wiring standards

Final Word

Net metering is one of the strongest financial drivers for going solar in Pakistan. A compliant design and proper paperwork can make a major difference in approval speed and savings.
