How Many Solar Panels Do You Need? Complete System Sizing Guide

20255 min read

Correct solar system sizing is the foundation of good ROI. Oversizing can increase payback time, while undersizing leaves large bills. The best approach starts with monthly energy units, appliance pattern, and rooftop conditions.

Step 1: Check Monthly Units (kWh)

Use the last 6–12 electricity bills to find average monthly units. This gives a stable baseline for system design.

Step 2: Estimate Required kW

A practical shortcut in many local scenarios is:

Required kW ≈ Monthly Units ÷ 110 (approximation, varies by city, season, and system losses).

Example: 1,100 units/month suggests about a 10kW class system.

Step 3: Convert kW to Panel Count

If using ~580W panels:

Panel Count ≈ System kW × 1000 ÷ 580

For 10kW: ~17–18 panels depending on exact module wattage and design margin.

Step 4: Validate Roof & Shading

  • Check usable roof area
  • Identify shading from nearby buildings/water tanks
  • Assess orientation and tilt

Step 5: Choose System Type

On-grid for strongest financial return, hybrid for backup + savings, and off-grid for no-grid locations.

Final Advice

Use bill data + site survey + professional design before purchase. Accurate sizing protects your ROI and performance for years.
